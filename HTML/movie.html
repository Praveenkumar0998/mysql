Soorarai Pottru

About this movie

Soorarai Pottru (transl.â€‰Praise the Brave) is a 2020 Indian Tamil-language drama film directed by Sudha Kongara, who wrote the screenplay with Shalini Usha Nair. The film, co-produced by Suriya and Jyothika of 2D Entertainment and Guneet Monga of Sikhya Entertainment, stars Suriya, Aparna Balamurali and Paresh Rawal, while Mohan Babu, Urvashi and Karunas appear in supporting roles. The film was inspired by events from the life of G. R. Gopinath, founder of Indian low-cost airline Simplifly Deccan, as described in his memoir Simply Fly: A Deccan Odyssey.The project was announced in mid-2018 under the working title Suriya 38, and the official title was announced in April 2019. Principal photography began the same month and ended that September, and filming took place in Madurai, Chennai, and Raigarh. G. V. Prakash Kumar composed the film's music, while Niketh Bommireddy was the cinematographer and Sathish Suriya edited the film.The theatrical release of the film was affected by post-production delays and the COVID-19 pandemic; it was released through Amazon Prime Video on 12 November 2020, the eve of the Diwali festival, and received critical acclaim. It was selected as one of ten Indian films to be screened in the Best Foreign Film category at the 78th Golden Globe Awards. The film also entered the Panorama Section of the Shanghai International Film Festival. It won five awards at the 68th National Film Awards: Best Feature Film, Best Actor (Suriya), Best Actress (Aparna), Best Screenplay (Sudha and Nair) and Best Background Score (Prakash Kumar). A Hindi remake Sarfira, again directed by Sudha, was released in 2024.

Casting

Suriya played the role of Nedumaaran Rajangam (Maara), an ambitious man from Madurai; the character is loosely based on G. R. Gopinath.[18] He said his character "is prone to anger ... Even in anger, he is able to think of why certain people are coming from a different perspective. He knows how to behave with his wife, his friends and others". Sudha fixed these elements before filming to make the performance look consistent.[17] He also did few physical transformations;[19][20][21] For scenes of the character as a young man, Suriya had lost almost 20 kg (44 lb) to gain physique for the role.[22] Suriya plays Nedumaaran Rajangam and Aparna Balamurali plays Sundari. Their characters are based on G. R. Gopinath and his wife Bhargavi. Aparna Balamurali was signed to play the female lead, working with Suriya for the first time.[23][24] Her character Sundari was modelled on Gopinath's wife Bhargavi.[25][26] Aparna also learnt the Madurai dialect for the film.[24] In June 2019, Telugu actor Mohan Babu was signed to appear in the film.[27] He immediately accepted the role when approached, and dubbed in his own voice.[28] Soorarai Pottru is also the acting debut of Arjunan's son Ilan and daughter Iyal.[29] Bollywood actor Paresh Rawal was cast as the antagonist Paresh Goswami, making his Tamil debut.[30] The character is based on Naresh Goyal, CEO of Jet Airways.[31] Dan Dhanoa appeared in a short role as Vimal Balaiyya, which was inspired by Indian businessman Vijay Mallya.[32] Sheik Maideen, who had a close resemblance to former president of India A. P. J. Abdul Kalam and was popularly known as "Udumalai Kalam", played the character inspired by Kalam;[33] Maideen's voice was dubbed by Naveen Muralidhar.[34] R. Madhavan gave the narration for the film.[35]
